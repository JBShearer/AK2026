Quick Summary
Alignment: $1.7B procurement; Arkansas Forward procurement pillar; AI CoE Pillar 2 (detect vendor billing irregularities)
Expected Value: $2M-$5M annually; supports AI CoE Pillar 2
📊 Arkansas Context (Verified)
Arkansas processes $1.7 billion in annual procurement spend across 200+ state agencies through AASIS (Arkansas Administrative Statewide Information System), the state's SAP ERP platform operational since 1996. The Arkansas Forward initiative, a comprehensive efficiency study by McKinsey & Co. ($5.5M contract), identified procurement reform as the largest single savings opportunity, projecting $140M-$230M in potential savings. The February 2026 AI Center of Excellence Report explicitly recommends AI for "detecting vendor billing irregularities" as a Pillar 2 (Operational Efficiencies) priority. The state's decentralized procurement model—where agencies historically purchased independently—creates vulnerability to duplicate payments, shell company fraud, and vendor collusion that centralized AI-powered screening can address.
🎯 Problem Analysis
Without centralized fraud detection across the $1.7B annual procurement spend, Arkansas faces significant risk exposure. The state's 200+ agencies each manage vendor relationships independently, creating gaps in visibility for duplicate invoices submitted across agencies, shell companies with shared addresses or bank accounts, bid-rigging patterns across related procurements, and vendors debarred in other jurisdictions. The $19,000 lectern scandal in 2023 highlighted the political urgency for procurement transparency. Arkansas Forward's procurement pillar targets $140M-$230M in savings over six years—vendor fraud detection is foundational to achieving this target while protecting taxpayer dollars.
🏗️ BTP Solution Architecture

Recommended BTP Architecture:

  • SAP Business Integrity Screening as the core fraud detection engine, integrated with AASIS
  • SAP Business Data Cloud to consolidate vendor master data across all 200+ agencies for cross-agency pattern detection
  • SAP Work Zone, advanced edition to surface fraud alerts and investigation workflows to procurement officers
  • SAP Task Center for unified approval queues on flagged transactions requiring review
  • Custom Joule assistant trained on Arkansas procurement policies and debarment lists for natural language vendor inquiries

Quick Summary
Alignment: Core Arkansas Forward finding; AI CoE Pillar 2 (bulk purchasing, consolidation)
Expected Value: $10M-$20M annually; largest single savings opportunity
📊 Arkansas Context (Verified)
Arkansas Forward identified spend consolidation as the core procurement opportunity, noting that "the state doesn't capture benefit of scale across agencies." The McKinsey analysis documented $1.7 billion in annual procurement fragmented across 200+ agencies purchasing independently—the same supplies, services, and commodities often at different prices from different vendors. The recommendation: "consistent product categories, consolidated contracts, better RFP negotiation, deploy end-to-end e-procurement system." ARBuy, the state's new e-procurement modernization, provides the transactional foundation; AI-powered spend analytics enables the consolidation intelligence.

This fragmentation results in: price variance of 15-40% on common commodities, duplicative vendor relationships and contract administration costs, missed volume discount thresholds, and inability to identify maverick spending outside negotiated contracts. Arkansas Forward projects $140M-$230M in savings from procurement reform—spend consolidation is the largest component.
🏗️ BTP Solution Architecture

Recommended BTP Architecture:

  • SAP Ariba Spend Analysis with AI-powered category intelligence and consolidation opportunity identification
  • SAP Business Data Cloud as the cross-agency spend data harmonization layer, normalizing taxonomies across AASIS and ARBuy
  • SAP Work Zone executive dashboards showing spend by category, agency, and consolidation opportunity value
  • Custom Joule assistant for natural language spend queries ("What are we spending on office supplies across all agencies?")
